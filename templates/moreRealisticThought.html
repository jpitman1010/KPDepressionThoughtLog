More Realistic Questions




In reference to your automatic thought:

'{{ session['automatic_thought'] }}',

answer the following questions to help guide yourself toward more realistic thought processes.




  • Do I have any evidence to believe this (distorted) thought?





  • Is it reasonable to expect that I will never make mistakes?





  • Is it really "everybody", "always", "never", etc...?






  • If this happened once or a few times, does it necessarily mean forever?





  • Has anyone else faced a similar roadblock and eventually overcome it






  • Are there other things happening in my life that are not bad?



  • Are there things going alright or things to be thankful for?





  • Am I disqualifying this because it really is not true?




    Am I doing it out of habit because it’s hard for me to think of myself as OK, appealing, competent, etc.?




  • If it is a compliment, can I say “Thank you?”






  • Is it possible they are thinking something else?




  • Is there another possible explanation for why they said or did that?




  • Could their actions have more to do with themselves than me? Is it necessarily about me?




  • Have I checked it out with them??






  • What is the most LIKELY outcome (not the worst I can imagine)?



  • What are the odds that this thought: {{session['automatic_thought']}}, is really going to happen? **If the fear is inevidible, such as death, or getting a cold, what is the liklihood it will occur in the near future?** Select a percentage---










  • I were going to bet my next paycheck, would I bet on it happening or not happening?




  • Have I made it through difficult times before? Even if it did happen the way I imagine, would I survive?






  • How important is this in the grand scheme of my life?





  • Am I really going to care about this in 2 years? 2 months? 2 weeks?








    (a)'Shoulds' towards ourselves:

  • Is it realistic to expect myself to read the future or know in advance what was going to happen?




  • Is it reasonable to expect that I will always know the best way to respond or act in the moment?




  • If someone else made the same mistake, would I be as mean to them as I am to myself?




    • (b) 'Shoulds' towards others:

  • Is it reasonable to expect everybody to want what I want, act how I act, value what I value?




  • Is this really how it “should” be, or is it just my preference--the way I like it, what I prefer, or what works best for me?






  • Is that really my (or their) only attribute?





  • Is it possible that I (or they) have some good traits or qualities or skills besides the one I’m focusing on?







  • Does the other person share some responsibility?




  • If I do share some of the blame for how things turned out, what % of the responsibility should I take?













  • Although there are things I cannot control, is there some aspect of this situation that I can influence?




  • Is there something I can learn from the experience so I can have more influence the next time I’m in a similar situation?






  • Even if they said/did something that triggered me, is this something I was already sensitive about? Insecure about? Even before they did/said something?




  • Did I have these insecurities way before they ever came into the picture? Have I had these insecurities in other situations that had nothing to do with this person?


  • If I truly didn’t believe what they said, would they be able to create a mood in me?




  • Is it because they forced me to have a mood, or is it that I bought into what they were selling?






  • If I am wrong, does this really mean that I am somehow less than them?




  • This something that is really important (as far as how I see myself, how I experience the world, my safety, etc.) or is it just something to be right about?




  • What do I win if I’m proven right? Do the costs of arguing outweigh the benefits?







  • Is it reasonable to expect others to know my needs or try to meet my needs if I have never clearly expressed them?




  • Why would others strive to meet my needs if I have not held them accountable for doing so?




  • Am I striking a reasonable balance between meeting my own needs and meeting the needs of others?





  • Is this unfair event or situation something that I can realistically control? Would it be a good use of my energy to try to control this?




  • Though this situation is unfair, are there ways in which my life has also been fair, or even more than fair, in my favor?
